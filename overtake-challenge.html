Relative Motion (Overtake)

Relative motion helps us understand how one object moves with respect to another.

In overtaking:

  • One vehicle is faster
  • The other is slower
  • We observe motion from one vehicle's point of view
Key Idea

👉 What really matters is relative velocity — it tells how quickly the gap between two objects changes.

Formulas
REL. VEL.v_rel = v₁ − v₂
TIMEt = Distance / v_rel
DISTANCEd = v × t
Understanding
◆If v₁ > v₂ → overtaking happens
◆If v₁ = v₂ → no overtaking
◆Higher relative velocity → faster overtake
Insight

👉 Always convert speeds into the same units before solving.
