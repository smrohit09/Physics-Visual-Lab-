Wave Motion & Signals

What is a Wave?
A wave is a way of transferring energy through oscillations.
In this lab, we visualize waves like signals used in real systems.
Key Idea
👉 A wave is defined by how high it is, how fast it repeats, and how it is aligned.
Core Quantities
  • Amplitude (A) → strength of the wave
  • Frequency (f) → how many waves per second
  • Phase (φ) → alignment of waves
Formulas
y = A sin(2πft + φ) Wave Equation
v = fλ Wave Speed Relation
Understanding
  • Higher amplitude → stronger signal
  • Higher frequency → waves are closer together
  • Changing phase shifts the wave left or right
👉 Insight: Frequency controls spacing, amplitude controls height.
💡 Try adjusting amplitude and frequency and observe how the wave changes.
